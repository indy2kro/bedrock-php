# Bedrock PHP

## AI Rules for Profile Generation & Monetization

### Purpose of This Document

This document defines **how AI tools may be used** to generate, maintain, and extend **Bedrock PHP Profiles** (environment and constraint-specific interpretations), and how **monetization boundaries** are enforced through those rules.

Its purpose is to ensure that:

- Bedrock PHP **remains fully free and authoritative**
- Profiles remain **non-core, optional, and clearly scoped**
- AI-generated content does not erode structural, philosophical, or economic boundaries
- Monetization remains **intentional, defensible, and sustainable**

This document is normative for any AI-assisted work related to profiles.

---

## 1. Foundational Principle

**Bedrock PHP is free. Profiles are not.**

AI must treat:

- **Bedrock PHP Core** as immutable, public, and non-commercial
- **Profiles** as derivative, optional, and monetizable artifacts

AI must never blur this distinction.

---

## 2. Definition of a Profile

A *Profile* is a **constrained interpretation** of Bedrock PHP under a specific, real-world limitation.

Profiles:

- do not introduce new core rules
- do not weaken Bedrock PHP defaults
- do not redefine Bedrock PHP philosophy
- may *narrow*, *strengthen*, or *make explicit* existing rules

Profiles exist to answer:

> "What does Bedrock PHP look like *here*?"

They do not answer:

> "What is the best way to do X?"

---

## 3. Categories of Profiles (Monetization-Relevant)

AI may generate profiles **only** within the following categories.

### 3.1 Environment Profiles

Examples:

- Bedrock PHP for AWS
- Bedrock PHP for Azure
- Bedrock PHP for Self-Hosted Infrastructure

Rules:

- Each environment profile is a **separate paid artifact**
- AI may generate **one designated reference environment profile** for free distribution
- Any *additional* environment profile is considered paid content

AI must:

- explicitly state which cloud/provider assumptions apply
- list accepted managed services
- list explicitly rejected services or architectures
- define stop rules that prevent environment-driven complexity

AI must not:

- provide service catalogs
- provide IaC recipes
- promote provider-specific features

---

### 3.2 Scale & Team Size Profiles

Examples:

- Bedrock PHP for Small Teams (≤5 engineers)
- Bedrock PHP for Medium Teams (6–20 engineers)

Rules:

- Small Team profile may be distributed freely as a baseline reference
- Medium Team and larger profiles are **paid artifacts**

AI must:

- tighten defaults as team size increases
- introduce earlier stop rules
- increase bias toward predictability and deletion

AI must not:

- introduce coordination-heavy practices
- recommend process frameworks or management tooling

---

### 3.3 Longevity & Lifecycle Profiles

Examples:

- Bedrock PHP for Long-Lived Systems (5+ years)
- Bedrock PHP for Legacy PHP Systems

Rules:

- All longevity-focused profiles are **paid artifacts**
- These profiles assume staff turnover and loss of context

AI must:

- strengthen documentation and deletion rules
- bias toward conservative upgrades
- make operational survivability explicit

AI must not:

- promise modernization paths
- frame legacy as a deficiency

---

## 4. Free vs Paid Boundary Rules

### 4.1 Free Content (AI May Generate Freely)

AI may generate and publish for free:

- the full Bedrock PHP core specification
- one reference environment profile
- one small-team profile
- meta-documentation explaining what profiles are and why they exist

These free artifacts exist to:

- demonstrate intent
- allow evaluation
- prevent lock-in

---

### 4.2 Paid Content (AI Must Treat as Restricted)

AI-generated content **must be classified as paid** when it:

- targets more than one cloud provider
- assumes medium or large team sizes
- assumes long-lived or regulated systems
- documents real-world compromises and tradeoffs

AI must never:

- reproduce paid profile content verbatim in free outputs
- summarize paid profiles beyond their table of contents
- collapse multiple profiles into a single free document

---

## 5. AI Behavior Constraints

### 5.1 No Scope Expansion

AI must not:

- suggest new profile categories without human approval
- merge environment and scale profiles automatically
- introduce "enterprise" or "premium" framing

---

### 5.2 No Monetization Leakage

AI must not:

- frame paid profiles as necessary for adoption
- imply that Bedrock PHP is incomplete without profiles
- apply guilt, urgency, or fear framing

Correct framing:

> "Profiles are optional interpretations for teams operating under specific constraints."

---

### 5.3 Version Discipline

AI must:

- version profiles independently from Bedrock PHP core
- declare compatibility explicitly
- allow profiles to be deprecated or retired

AI must not:

- promise ongoing updates
- imply evergreen maintenance

---

## 6. Structural Requirements for AI-Generated Profiles

Every AI-generated profile must contain:

1. Scope & Assumptions
2. Unchanged Bedrock Defaults
3. Strengthened or Narrowed Rules
4. Explicit Rejections
5. Stop Rules
6. Applicability Exit Conditions

If any section cannot be written clearly, the profile must not be generated.

---

## 7. Final Constraint

AI exists to **compress hard-earned decisions**, not to create new ones.

If an AI-generated profile:

- introduces optionality
- adds choices
- weakens defaults
- or undermines the free core

…it must be rejected.

---

### Closing Statement

Bedrock PHP remains free so that it can remain authoritative.

Profiles are paid so that they can remain:

- honest
- bounded
- maintained
- and optional

AI must actively protect this boundary.
